## 4. Key design decisions (made — don't reopen)
1. **Amplitudes are scaled with μ, not just `mu_friction`.** μ is the *scale of the friction circle*;
   keeping μ=0.5 envelopes makes μ=0.8 under-utilized (μ unobservable) and μ=0.3 saturated. **The
   precise per-trajectory scaling factors are being supplied by the user** (with a formal justification
   from the analytical accel-envelope TeX doc, §3.4 eqs 27–35 / 33–35, "b-term" absorption) — encode
   them into the RULES dict of `gen_mu_scaled.jl` + `setup_mu_full.jl`.
2. **spin_creep and multisine are friction-loaded, not rate-bound** (the parent brief mis-classified
   them). Verified by `tracking_gate`: spin_creep sits at ~0.95 utilization and **violated the circle
   6.3 % at μ=0.3 unscaled**; multisine at 0.6–0.81. → **both must be scaled.** `long_circle` is the
   only truly low-load profile (util 0.01–0.07) → leave **unscaled**. octagon, coupled_vomega, ellipse,
   spiral_orbit scaling was piloted and validated.
3. **Row-preserving scaling**: combo_idx *i* at the new μ is the same design point with amplitudes
   scaled — so the μ groups stay matched for the downstream identifiability analysis.
4. **Generators were reconstructed** (the original `gen_*_toml.py` are absent from the repo);
   gold-checked: regenerating at μ=0.5 reproduces the original combos **byte-identical**.
5. **Suffix scheme**: μ-versioned TOMLs `<profile>_mu_0pN.toml` in one `profiles/` dir; `profiles.jl`
   strips the suffix so the **output profile id stays bare** and files land in the **shared data folder**.
6. **Arrow-only** (no JLD2 — storage; ~130 GB for both μ otherwise), **≤6 threads**, **seed 1234**.
7. **Always pilot before the full sweep**: generate the affected profiles' near-cap combos at the new μ,
   run `tracking_gate`, require **utilization matching the μ=0.5 band with no new `missed`**, then sweep.
